Editing

Here is a collection of my proudest documents that I have edited. These documents include an essay I edited for my internship with the scholarly journal Material Culture, a letter to the author of a short story I edited and critiqued, a document I created with the sole purpose to practice my editing at a glance, and a short story that I wrote and then edited for publication.

Roth Essay

For my internship, I edited Jeffery E. Roth's essay on the historic, and controversial, landscaping found in New Mexico and Texas called long lots. This document showcases my proficiency using Microsoft Word's Track Changes. I used Words into Type Volume 3 to edit this essay.

Author Letter

As a final project, my editing professor challenged us to read and edit any kind of document and craft a letter to the author to mimic what editoral critique would look like in the work place. I chose to edit and critique the short story Working Hands by my friend and fellow author Alyssa Minch. I used Words into Type Volume 3 and Track Changes to edit this short story, which you can view by following the link located in the title of the story.

Vulture Project

What started out as a homework, turned into one of my all time favorite projects. To quote the mastermind behind the project, Dr. Bill Williamson, "The Vulture Project is a collection of communication mishaps from the world around us. The Vulture Project challenges you to recognize and assess grammatical and stylistic issues at a glance, and to consider how an editor might assist in alleviating the problems."

Our Masked Hero

This is a short story that I wrote about Mason, a man stuck with a boring job, in a boring routine, and a boring life. Enter The Masked Hero, a new superhero fighting for jusice on the streets of New York. Mason becomes obessed with the identity of The Masked Hero. Obsession turns escapism; Mason doesn't want to face his own shortcomings.
This short story uses Track Changes and I used Words into Type Volume 3 to edit this story.
